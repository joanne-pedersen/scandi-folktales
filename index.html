Joe Hays

Freelance Web Developer and Graphic Designer

My name is Joe Hays and I am a technology enthusiast. Even before I knew the alphabet I had my hands on a keyboard. Although I enjoy video games, my passion lies in multiple facets of the technology industry. Learning about the latest innovations in mobile technology, including smart phones, virtual reality, augmented reality, and wearables increases my understanding of the direction of the future of technology. After my initial class of "Intro to Web Design" during my sophomore year in high school, I fell in love with web design and development. I have recently branched out into other aspects of web development such as graphic design and program coding. I have achieved four certifications so far: Adobe Certified Associate in Web Communications using Adobe Dreamweaver© CS6, Adobe Certified Associate using Adobe Photoshop© CS6, Testout PC Pro, and Testout Network Pro. Through my classes at Summit Technology Academy and Lee's Summit North High School, I participated in the 2014-15 US Cyberpatriot Competitions, and was a state finalist in the FBLA E-commerce Web Development competition.

Graphics Music Photos

Towards the end of 2014 we were tasked to create a website for FBLA. We divided our class into two groups of three. My team took on the E-Commerce Website. For this website we used Photoshop, Dreamweaver, and Brackets to code and design the website to the clients needs. In this website we needed to create a shopping cart system that allows the customer to purchase items directly from the website. We also needed to set up a way for the customer to contact the artist. We decided to use Bootstrap by Twitter as our framework to enable mobile responsiveness allowing for a better experience on handheld devices. We started by designing our layout. We wanted a showcase for the artists creations as well as link to their website. I think that had we used our time more effectivly we could have produced a more visualy pleasing website. Overall I think the website did fairly well and we earned 5th place in the state competition.

Starry Night Courtwarming

A couple of weeks before the Courtwarming game our Web Design Team was divided into three teams of two. We were told to create a website with all the information for all of the events surrounding courtwarming. We were given the theme "Starry Night" for the dance and we used that as our theme for the website. After planing the website out on paper we started to create the website from scratch using Brackets, Photoshop and the Bootstrap framework by Twitter. Withen I did the coding and my partner did all of our graphics. Once all of the teams were done we decided as a group which teams website would be posted. Despite some minor mistakes I think this website is one of my favorites. This was the website that was posted to the live server which was quite the honor for me and my partner.

Hydra Flag

Ever since I started graphic design I have had a full head of new fun designs to create and this was one of them. Using Photoshop I took a base "Hydra" logo and trased it with a pen to recreate it in a vector style allowing it to enlarge without distorting. Summit Slam 2015

Every year Lee's Summit North hosts a tennis tournament and has a competition for the design of the shirt. This was my submition to that contest. Using Photoshop I Typed the words "Summit Slam" and then removed the "A" in "slam" allowing for some creative work. From there I made a tennis racquet and tennis ball in the schools colors of Chrimson and Sliver. I then made a trail behind the ball giving it the feel that it had just been hit by the racquet. I did this to create a sort of lower case "a" to finish the word "slam".

Modern Design #2

In Advanced Web Design we created a website for Future Business Leaders of America. This website was for "three young entrepreneurial artists" so we needed some art. I created a set of Material Design inspired artwork to sell on this website. Using Photoshop I started with the background layer and added another rectangle to it at an angle. I continued to do this 4 times slightly varying the color each time. I then added some noise to a few layers to give it some texture. Then I added a drop shadow to create depth withen the picture. Although this is only my second art of this style I still consiter it my favorite.

More About Me

I was born in 1998 to Frank and Ruth Hays. My older brother, Ben, has been a huge inspiration and a friend to me all my life. We have 2 dogs: Otis and Charlie, and a cat named Meow. As a young child I had an obsession with Legos™, phones, gadgets and computers. My parents will attest to my unending imagination and intense creativity. Pretending to be the Master Spy of the World, I would spend hours inventing make-believe tools and weapons to rid the world of "bad guys". My parents endured and encouraged my overactive imagination, and I benefit from their belief in me to this day. In an attempt to focus my energy, I was involved in Tae-Kwon-Do and received my black belt rank in 2008. I began Cub Scouts as a first grader and crossed over to Boy Scouts. In March 2014, I achieved the rank of Eagle Scouts in Boy Scouts of America in Troop 251.

Even as a young child, technology had a huge impact on my life. Obtaining what my mom called "Junk" from local garage sales, I would take apart and reconfigure old electronic devices. I took apart an old, broken computer and, even to my dismay, I got it to work! I was hooked! The summer after my high school freshman year I took an online class, Computer Hardware and Operating Systems I, to test my interest and possible career choice. The following summer I was enrolled in Computer Hardware and Operating Systems II. I was accepted into the Missouri Innovation Campus program at Summit Technology Academy where I, as a junior, studied network configuration and designed in th class IT/Network Essentials. With the help of my cousin, I built my first computer in 2013. Since them I have continued to upgrade my system and workspace with the addition of a new sound system, a couple monitors, and even another computer. True to my previous antics, I continued to obtain most of my items at little to no cost through garage sales and thrift shops. To this day my mom cringes when she sees me bring in yet another box.
